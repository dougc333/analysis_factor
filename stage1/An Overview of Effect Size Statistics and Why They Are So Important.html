But when the variable is statistically significant, did you ever stop and ask yourself how significant it is? How large of an impact does it actually have on our outcome variable? The American Psychological Association expects all published research to answer that last question. But what statistics answer that? This webinar will cover that topic and more. In the webinar, we will go beyond “statistical significance” to “practical significance,” and we will consider “how big of a difference” rather than “is there a difference.” You probably know that the size of our sample can have a very large impact on the p-value of a predictor variable. We’ll discuss how using effect size statistics will help us avoid the interpretational quagmire created by sample size. In addition, we’ll discuss the "two families" of effect size, the /d family/ (differences between groups) and the /r family/ (measures of association). We’ll learn how to measure and interpret their results.
